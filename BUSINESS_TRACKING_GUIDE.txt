1. OVERVIEW — MONEY FLOW SUMMARY
================================================================================

Your money flows through this pipeline:

  MONEY GOES OUT                         MONEY COMES BACK
  ──────────────                         ─────────────────
  Buy raw paddy (Procurement)     →      Sell finished product (Sales)
  Pay for drying (Drying Process) →      Collect payment (Cash/GCash/COD)
  Mill into rice (Processing)     →      Delivery fees (if delivery order)
                 ↓
  Package into products (Distribution)
                 ↓
  Products ready for sale (Stock)

  FORMULA:
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Profit = Revenue (from sales) − Production Cost          │
  │                                                          │
  │ Production Cost = Procurement Cost + Drying Cost         │
  │ Revenue = Σ (sold items × selling price) - discounts     │
  │                + delivery fees                           │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

2. SCENARIO: PROCUREMENT TO SALES (Step by Step)
================================================================================

Here's a REAL scenario showing how ₱50,000 worth of paddy becomes sellable rice:

STEP 1: PROCUREMENT (Buy Raw Paddy)
────────────────────────────────────
  You buy raw paddy from a supplier:
    Supplier:     Juan's Farm
    Quantity:     1,000 kg of paddy
    Price/kg:     ₱25.00
    Total Cost:   ₱25,000

  → System creates a Procurement record
  → Status: Pending
  → Your money spent so far: ₱25,000

STEP 2: DRYING (Dry the Paddy)
──────────────────────────────
  You send the paddy for drying:
    Drying Method:   Machine drying
    Sacks:           40
    Price/sack/day:  ₱50
    Days:            3
    Drying Cost:     40 × ₱50 × 3 = ₱6,000

  → System creates a DryingProcess record
  → Status: Drying → Dried
  → Your money spent so far: ₱25,000 + ₱6,000 = ₱31,000

STEP 3: PROCESSING / MILLING (Mill into Rice)
──────────────────────────────────────────────
  The dried paddy goes through milling:
    Input:       1,000 kg dried paddy
    Output:      600 kg milled rice (≈60% yield is normal)
    Byproducts:  bran, husk (not tracked in system)

  → System creates a Processing record
  → Status: Processing → Completed
  → output_kg = 600
  → No additional cost tracked here (milling cost = your own operation)

STEP 4: DISTRIBUTION (Package into Products)
─────────────────────────────────────────────
  You distribute the milled rice into sellable products:
    Product:      Premium Rice 25kg    → 20 bags (500 kg)
    Product:      Premium Rice 5kg     → 20 bags (100 kg)
    Total:        600 kg distributed

  What the system calculates for each product:

  Premium Rice 25kg (20 bags):
    Cost traced:  ₱31,000 × (500/600) = ₱25,833.33
    Cost/unit:    ₱25,833.33 / 20 = ₱1,291.67 per bag
    Selling price: ₱1,500 per bag
    Profit/unit:  ₱1,500 − ₱1,291.67 = ₱208.33
    Margin:       13.9%

  Premium Rice 5kg (20 bags):
    Cost traced:  ₱31,000 × (100/600) = ₱5,166.67
    Cost/unit:    ₱5,166.67 / 20 = ₱258.33 per bag
    Selling price: ₱350 per bag
    Profit/unit:  ₱350 − ₱258.33 = ₱91.67
    Margin:       26.2%

  → System creates StockLog records with ALL cost fields populated
  → Products now have stock available for sale

STEP 5: SELLING (Create Orders / POS)
──────────────────────────────────────
  Customer buys from POS or delivery order:
    5× Premium Rice 25kg  = 5 × ₱1,500 = ₱7,500
    10× Premium Rice 5kg  = 10 × ₱350  = ₱3,500
    Subtotal:  ₱11,000
    Discount:  −₱500
    Delivery:  +₱200
    ─────────────────
    TOTAL:     ₱10,700

  → System creates a Sale record (status: pending)
  → When processed (pending → processing): stock is DEDUCTED
  → When delivered/completed: counted as REVENUE

STEP 6: PAYMENT COLLECTION
──────────────────────────
  Payment methods and when money is collected:

  Cash     → Paid immediately at POS          → payment_status = 'paid'
  GCash    → Paid immediately (with proof)    → payment_status = 'paid'
  COD      → Paid on delivery                 → payment_status = 'not_paid' until marked paid
  Pay Later → Paid at a future date           → payment_status = 'not_paid' until marked paid

  → Admin can mark unpaid orders as "Paid" in the Orders page
  → paid_at timestamp is recorded when payment is confirmed

3. TRACKING YOUR MONEY — WHERE IT GOES AND COMES BACK
================================================================================

MONEY OUT (Expenses):
─────────────────────
  ┌─────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Expense Type            │ Where It's Tracked                       │
  ├─────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Raw material (paddy)    │ procurements.total_cost                  │
  │                         │ = quantity_kg × price_per_kg             │
  ├─────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Drying cost             │ drying_processes.total_price             │
  │                         │ = sacks × price × days                   │
  ├─────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Per-unit production cost│ stock_logs.cost_per_unit                 │
  │                         │ = (procurement_cost + drying_cost) / qty │
  └─────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Note: Milling/processing operational costs (electricity, labor, machine
  maintenance) are NOT tracked in the system. These are your overhead costs
  that you should track separately.

MONEY IN (Revenue):
───────────────────
  ┌─────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Income Type             │ Where It's Tracked                       │
  ├─────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Product sales           │ sales.total (per order)                  │
  │                         │ = subtotal - discount + delivery_fee     │
  ├─────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Delivery fees           │ sales.delivery_fee                       │
  ├─────────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Total revenue           │ Dashboard: SUM of delivered/completed    │
  │                         │ sales only (voided/returned excluded)    │
  └─────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────────────────┘

PROFIT TRACKING:
────────────────
  Per-product profit is calculated at DISTRIBUTION time and saved in stock_logs:
    profit_per_unit = selling_price − cost_per_unit
    profit_margin   = (profit_per_unit / selling_price) × 100

  You can check this in:
    → Products page → Cost Analysis section (per product)
    → Stock distribution modal (shows cost breakdown before you distribute)

4. TRACKING INCOME & REVENUE
================================================================================

WHAT COUNTS AS REVENUE:
  ✅ Delivered orders     → counted
  ✅ Completed orders     → counted
  ❌ Pending orders       → NOT counted (not yet fulfilled)
  ❌ Processing orders    → NOT counted (in progress)
  ❌ Shipped orders       → NOT counted (not confirmed received)
  ❌ Voided orders        → NOT counted (cancelled/reversed)
  ❌ Returned orders      → NOT counted (returned by customer)

DASHBOARD STATS:
  Total Revenue         = SUM of all delivered + completed order totals
  Current Month Revenue = Same, filtered to current month
  Average Order Value   = Total Revenue / Number of fulfilled orders
  Total Items Sold      = SUM of item quantities from fulfilled orders
  Month-over-Month %    = ((this month − last month) / last month) × 100

PAYMENT STATUS TRACKING:
  The system tracks whether you've actually RECEIVED the money:

  payment_status = 'paid'     → Money received
  payment_status = 'not_paid' → Money not yet received (COD / Pay Later)

  IMPORTANT: An order being "delivered" does NOT mean you've been paid!
  For COD orders, the driver collects payment — admin must mark as "Paid"
  in the system to confirm the money was received.

  Filter orders by payment status in the Orders page to see:
    → Which delivered orders are still unpaid
    → Which customers owe you money

5. MANAGING RETURNED STOCK — DAMAGED, LOST, OR UNSELLABLE ITEMS
================================================================================

When a customer returns items, you have TWO choices:

OPTION A: RESTOCK (Item is still sellable)
──────────────────────────────────────────
  If the returned product is in good condition:
    1. Customer requests return → status: return_requested
    2. You accept & assign pickup driver → status: picking_up
    3. Driver picks up & you mark returned → status: returned
    4. Go to the returned order → click "Restock Items"
    5. Select WHICH items to restock and HOW MANY
    6. Stock is added back to inventory

  Example:
    Customer returned 3× Premium Rice 25kg
    All 3 bags are in good condition
    → Restock all 3 → stock goes from 15 to 18

OPTION B: DON'T RESTOCK (Item is damaged / unsellable)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  If the returned product is damaged, spoiled, or unsellable:
    1. Complete the return flow (steps 1-3 above)
    2. Do NOT restock those items (skip step 4, or restock only the good ones)
    3. The non-restocked items are effectively WRITTEN OFF as a loss

  Example:
    Customer returned 3× Premium Rice 25kg
    1 bag is fine, 2 bags are damaged (torn, wet, etc.)
    → Restock only 1 → stock goes from 15 to 16
    → The 2 damaged bags are YOUR LOSS

PARTIAL RESTOCK (Some items ok, some damaged):
───────────────────────────────────────────────
  The system supports per-item selective restocking:
    - You can restock specific items and specific quantities
    - Each item can only be restocked ONCE (no double-restocking)
    - Restocked quantity is capped at the original order quantity

  Example:
    Order had: 5× Rice 25kg + 10× Rice 5kg
    Customer returns everything
    After inspection:
      → Restock 5× Rice 25kg (all good)
      → Restock 7× Rice 5kg (3 bags damaged)
      → 3× Rice 5kg bags = YOUR LOSS

6. UNDERSTANDING YOUR LOSSES
================================================================================

TYPES OF LOSSES IN YOUR BUSINESS:
──────────────────────────────────

  1. DAMAGED RETURNS (Not restocked)
     ─────────────────────────────────
     What: Customer returned items that are damaged/unsellable
     Impact: You lose the production cost of those items
     How to calculate:
       Loss = non-restocked quantity × cost_per_unit (from stock_logs)
     Where to see: Compare returned order's items vs restocked items
       → Items NOT restocked = your loss

     Example:
       2 bags not restocked × ₱1,291.67 cost/unit = ₱2,583.34 loss

  2. VOIDED SALES
     ──────────────
     What: Order was cancelled/voided after processing (stock already deducted)
     Impact: No direct financial loss (stock is restored)
     BUT: If items were perishable or time-sensitive, the delay is indirect cost
     Note: Voiding ALWAYS restores ALL items to stock

  3. CANCELLED ORDERS (After Processing)
     ─────────────────────────────────────
     What: Order cancelled after stock was already deducted
     Impact: Stock is restored, but you lost time/effort
     No direct financial loss in the system

  4. UNPAID ORDERS (COD / Pay Later that never pay)
     ────────────────────────────────────────────────
     What: Delivered orders where payment was never collected
     Impact: You gave away products without receiving money
     How to track: Filter Orders by payment_status = 'not_paid'
     Your loss = order total of unpaid delivered orders

  5. PRODUCTION LOSSES (Yield loss during milling)
     ───────────────────────────────────────────────
     What: Not all paddy becomes rice (60-65% yield is normal)
     Impact: Built into your cost calculation automatically
     The system tracks input_kg vs output_kg in Processing
     Bran, husk, and other byproducts are not tracked

  6. UNTRACKED OVERHEAD COSTS
     ─────────────────────────
     What: Electricity, labor, machine maintenance, fuel, packaging
     Impact: These reduce your ACTUAL profit but aren't in the system
     Recommendation: Track these separately and subtract from system profit

HOW TO MANAGE DAMAGED GOODS — BEST PRACTICE:
─────────────────────────────────────────────

  When you receive returned items:

  Step 1: INSPECT each item thoroughly
  Step 2: SEPARATE into two piles: sellable vs unsellable
  Step 3: RESTOCK only the sellable items in the system
  Step 4: DO NOT RESTOCK the damaged items
          → They are your loss. The system accounts for this by:
             - The sale's revenue is NOT counted (returned order)
             - The stock is NOT restored for damaged items
             - Your cost_per_unit on those items is effectively lost

  Step 5: DOCUMENT the damage
          → Use the return notes and return proof images
          → Take photos of damaged items before disposal
          → This creates a paper trail for your records

  IS IT YOUR LOSS?
  ────────────────
  YES — damaged returned items that cannot be restocked are YOUR LOSS as the
  business owner. This is a normal cost of doing business. The system handles
  this gracefully:

    Revenue side:  Returned orders are NOT counted as revenue
    Stock side:    Only items you explicitly restock go back to inventory
    Cost side:     The production cost of non-restocked items is absorbed

  Your actual loss per damaged item = cost_per_unit from the stock distribution
  (procurement cost + drying cost, divided by units produced)

  If you want to track total losses over time, compare:
    → Total items in returned orders vs Total items restocked
    → The DIFFERENCE × average cost_per_unit = your total return losses

SUMMARY — QUICK REFERENCE
================================================================================

  Procurement → Drying → Processing → Distribution → Stock → Sale → Revenue

  Revenue tracked:  only delivered/completed orders
  Profit formula:   selling_price − cost_per_unit = profit_per_unit
