================================================================================
                    KJP RICE MILL MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
                     SYSTEM OBJECTIVES (CAPSTONE)
================================================================================


GENERAL OBJECTIVE:
    To develop a web-based rice mill management system for KJP Rice Mill
    that automates and streamlines the end-to-end rice supply chain — from
    procurement of raw palay through drying, processing, and inventory
    management to sales, order fulfillment, and delivery — while providing
    a public-facing e-commerce website and multi-role portals for
    administrators, staff, clients, and drivers.


SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES:

1.  To manage the procurement of raw palay from multiple suppliers,
    tracking quantity (kilograms and sacks), price per kilogram,
    total cost, and procurement status with support for inline
    supplier creation during procurement entry.

2.  To organize procurements into batches by rice variety with
    auto-generated batch numbers, enabling bulk tracking of
    total sacks, total kilograms, remaining quantities, and
    batch-level status management.

3.  To manage and monitor the drying process in two modes —
    individual procurement drying and batch drying with
    proportional sack distribution across member procurements —
    tracking drying days, cost per sack, and drying status with
    day increment and postponement capabilities.

4.  To manage the rice milling (processing) stage with multi-source
    drying input support, recording input and output quantities,
    automatically computing yield percentage and husk waste, and
    distributing finished output to product inventory upon
    completion.

5.  To manage rice product inventory with automated stock
    computation from completed processing output, stock floor
    thresholds for low-stock alerts, and a comprehensive stock
    log that records every inventory movement with source
    traceability.

6.  To monitor stock levels in real time and provide low-stock
    and out-of-stock alerts, inventory in/out movement charts,
    historical stock growth tracking, and per-batch cost
    breakdowns for better inventory visibility.

7.  To manage rice product varieties with name, description,
    and color attributes, ensuring variety traceability from
    procurement through batching, drying, processing, to
    finished product.

8.  To manage supplier records with validated contact information
    (name, contact person, phone, email, address) and maintain
    a per-supplier procurement history.

9.  To manage customer records with validated contact information,
    email verification via verification codes, customer account
    creation with login credentials, and a per-customer order
    history.

10. To provide an administrative dashboard with key performance
    indicators (revenue, orders, customers, products, stock
    totals), revenue trend charts (daily, weekly, monthly,
    bi-annual, annual), processing pipeline visualization,
    inventory health breakdown, payment method distribution,
    order status summaries, and recent activity feeds.

11. To provide a client portal where customers can browse
    products, search and filter by variety, manage a shopping
    cart with quantity controls and promo code support, view
    pricing breakdowns (subtotal, discount, delivery fee,
    total), and place orders online.

12. To provide a driver portal where delivery personnel can
    view assigned deliveries with priority indicators, start
    and complete deliveries, report failed deliveries, add
    delivery notes with photo/camera capture, and track
    personal delivery performance statistics.

13. To provide a staff portal with a Point of Sale (POS) module
    for processing walk-in transactions, a daily sales summary
    dashboard, low-stock alert monitoring, stock distribution
    visualization, and a recent activity feed.

14. To manage sales and orders through an 11-status workflow
    (Pending, Processing, Shipped, Delivered, Completed,
    Return Requested, Picking Up, Returned, Cancelled, Voided)
    with automatic stock deduction on processing and stock
    restoration on cancellation.

15. To support multiple payment methods (Cash, GCash, Cash on
    Delivery, Pay Later) with payment proof upload capability,
    payment status tracking, and a password-protected void
    transaction feature with audit logging.

16. To manage delivery assignments with auto-generated delivery
    numbers, driver selection, customer destination tracking,
    priority levels (Low, Normal, High, Urgent), delivery
    status progression (Pending, In Transit, Delivered), and
    proof-of-delivery documentation.

17. To integrate address autocomplete and distance calculation
    using the OpenRouteService API for estimating delivery
    distances from the warehouse and computing delivery fees
    based on configurable shipping rates.

18. To implement a return and refund management system that
    supports return requests with reason and proof submission,
    admin acceptance or rejection, driver assignment for pickup
    scheduling, return completion tracking, and selective item
    restocking back into inventory.

19. To manage staff records including name, email, phone, role
    assignment, position (Secretary, Driver), position-specific
    fields (truck plate number for drivers), date hired, and
    active/inactive status.

20. To manage driver records with license number, vehicle type,
    plate number, contact details, delivery statistics, and
    active/inactive status tracking.

21. To implement role-based access control with five distinct
    user roles — Super Admin, Admin, Staff, Client, and Driver —
    each with separate portals, navigation structures, and
    permission-scoped API access enforced through Sanctum
    token-based authentication with role abilities.

22. To implement a Content Management System (CMS) that allows
    the admin to update the public website's home page hero
    section, about page content, and contact information
    without requiring code changes.

23. To provide a public-facing website with a Home page
    (hero section, feature highlights, company statistics,
    testimonials), About page (mission, vision, values,
    company timeline, team profiles), Products page
    (browsable product catalog with stock status), and
    Contact page (business information, inquiry form with
    categorized inquiry types, FAQ section).

24. To provide a customizable appearance and theme system
    allowing the admin to configure primary, secondary, and
    button colors for personalizing the look and feel of the
    system with dark mode support.

25. To provide a business settings module for managing business
    name, logo upload, contact details, phone number, address,
    operating hours, and social media links (Facebook, Twitter,
    Instagram, LinkedIn).

26. To provide configurable shipping settings including rate
    per sack, rate per kilometer, base kilometer threshold,
    and warehouse address for automated delivery fee
    calculation.

27. To provide an audit trail that logs all system activities
    (create, update, delete, login, logout, archive, restore)
    with user identification, IP address, user agent, action
    description, and detailed JSON change records for full
    accountability and transparency.

28. To implement a database backup and export feature supporting
    full SQL database export, database statistics viewing,
    CSV data export by module, and CSV data import for data
    security, recovery, and migration.

29. To implement a responsive web design that adapts seamlessly
    across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices with skeleton
    loaders, toast notifications, and optimistic UI updates
    for a smooth user experience.

30. To implement data visualization through interactive charts
    (line charts for trends, bar charts for comparisons, donut
    charts for distributions) and sortable, paginated data
    tables across all dashboards and management pages for
    data-driven decision making.

31. To implement a cost traceability system that tracks
    production costs from procurement (price per kilogram)
    through drying (cost per sack per day) and processing to
    compute per-unit cost, selling price comparison, profit
    per unit, and profit margin percentage via the stock log.

32. To implement predictive sales analytics using weighted
    moving average and linear regression to forecast revenue
    trends (daily, monthly, yearly), product demand predictions,
    and stock status projections with configurable forecast
    horizons and cache-backed performance.

33. To implement a two-tier archiving system — Tier 1 (archive)
    for soft-archiving records with restoration capability and
    Tier 2 (soft delete) for permanent removal — with bulk
    operations, archive statistics, and module-level archive
    management accessible to Super Admins.

34. To implement product image management allowing the admin to
    upload, update, and display product photos across the
    system, POS interface, client shop, and public-facing
    product catalog pages.

35. To implement a server-side caching strategy using Redis with
    5-minute TTL and automatic cache invalidation on data
    mutations, paired with a client-side dual-layer cache
    (memory and localStorage) with stale-while-revalidate
    pattern for optimized system performance.
